About Us

Our students are the leaders of tomorrow: equipped to innovate, and ready to thrive in any workplace.

Research shows that organizations with the ability to recruit and include employees from diverse backgrounds benefit from an advantage that their competitors do not experience. These companies are more likely to see an increase in creativity, problem solving, and flexible adaptation, which lends to a more innovative approach market changes.

Employtech provides internship placements into companies, working with nontraditional university students as a way to provide employers with access to diverse talent. This includes Underrepresented, Transfers, Re-entry, and Formerly Incarcerated students. Our flagship program is based at UC Berkeley, offering a semester-long training stream. This is an experience of a series of workshops that introduces practical soft skills concepts to hand-selected Fellows to equip them with the ability to thrive in competitive internships.

Employtech has teamed up with 3 Queens Design to introduce IDEO’s Human Centered Design (HCD) methodology to Social Science and Humanities students as a framework for innovative problem-solving in the business world.

Vision

Employtech is seeking to improve the economic and social outcomes of higher education among students that come from underserved populations while encouraging them to re-invest in their home communities.

Many of our students have overcome circumstances that presented barriers that made graduating from college seem near impossible, with pressing financial responsibilities that broaden the income gaps that they face compared to their “traditional” counterparts. We believe that providing our Fellows with access to the “hidden curriculum” that many underserved students are unaware of will equip them with the ability to experience the upward mobility that higher education can provide.

Our key areas of focus are in training, mentorship and internship placement.

Mission

Our mission is to lend to innovation within corporate spaces through mindful diversity & inclusion as we empower nontraditional students with the soft skills they need to thrive in the transition from academia to their professional careers.

Program Details

Employtech is where top students prepare to exceed the expectations of amazing companies.

Employers today expect that recent college graduates have exposure to applied learning experiences that enable problem-solving through collaboration, research, and community engagement in order to effective in the workplace. Each Employtech fellow is selected from various Social Science and Humanities majors, leveraging the critical thinking and empathy skills that they are taught in their existing coursework.

  • Students who have a history of performance and leadership are recruited to interview for the program and vetted based on both their aptitude for the WEF Character Qualities, and requirements that companies have expressed.
  • Students are enrolled as part of a cohort of fellows into a rigorous training stream that introduces problem-based learning through an assigned social issue that must be solved as a final project. We teach fellows to employ Human Centered Design as a way to solve real-world problems by putting the “user” first.
  • Throughout the course, Fellows are gaining tools for and employing Competencies skills in addition to participating in workshops that introduce varying perspectives of the business world-examples include an introduction to startups, understanding the power of quantitative analysis, and how to effectively apply entrepreneurial skills in a big company.
  • Fellows are assigned selected mentors as part of the program for support and guidance throughout their careers.
  • Fellows present their solutions to a panel of business professionals as their final project.
  • Fellows are assigned internships within companies as an opportunity to translate academic experience into assisting business units within organizations that have a need for effective and learned interns.
