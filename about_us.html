About Us

A human-centered approach makes social awareness a competitive advantage.

Research shows that organizations with the ability to recruit employees from diverse backgrounds are more likely to see an increase in creativity, problem solving, and flexible adaptation, lending to innovative approaches to market changes.

Employtech introduces diverse talent to employers by providing access to “nontraditional” students. These includes a range of underrepresented students that are transfers, re-entry, veterans and student parents, and offer a wealth of perspectives.

We have teamed up with 3 Queens Design to introduce Human-Centered Design (HCD) methodology as a framework for innovative problem-solving. Our flagship program is based at UC Berkeley, offering a series of workshops that introduces practical soft skills to hand-selected fellows.

Vision and Values

Employtech is seeking to improve the economic and social outcomes of higher education among students that come from underserved populations while encouraging them to re-invest in their home communities.

Many of our students are low income, first generation college, or system-impacted and have overcome circumstances that presented barriers that made completing thier education seem near impossible. We believe that providing our fellows with access to the hidden curriculum that many underserved students are unaware of will equip them with the ability to experience the upward mobility that higher education can provide.

Additionally, program participants are expected to contribute to their own communities through mentorship, economic investment and education.

Our key areas of focus are in training, mentorship and internship placement.

Mission

Our mission is to lend to innovation in business by empowering nontraditional students with the 21st Century skills necessary to thrive in their professional careers.

Program Details

Employtech is where incredible students prepare to exceed the expectations of amazing companies.

Employers today face the challenge of finding college graduates whose exposure to applied learning experiences enable problem-solving through collaboration, research, and community engagement in order to effective in the workplace.

Each Employtech fellow is selected from various Social Science and Humanities majors, leveraging the critical thinking and empathy skills that they are taught in their existing coursework:

  • Future fellows are vetted based on the World Economic Forum’s Character Qualities
  • Fellows learn to employ Human-Centered Design to solve real-world problems
  • Fellows work on a social issue to solve while employing competencies skills
  • Fellows are assigned selected mentors for career support and guidance
  • Final projects are presented to a panel of professionals
  • Fellows are assigned internships within companies
  • Fellows become mentors to future cohorts
