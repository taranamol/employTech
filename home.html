Employtech provides internship placements into companies, working with nontraditional university students as a way to provide employers with access to untapped talent.

In 2015, the World Economic Forum published a report acknowledging the skills gap between academia and the digital economy, and stating that social and emotional skills are critical for employees to meaningfully contribute to workplaces of the 21st century. This includes 10 proficiencies labeled as either “competencies” or “character qualities”, and “allow creativity, problem solving and communication and have at their heart social interactions”. Competencies include creativity, critical thinking/ problem solving, collaboration and communication, and the Character Qualities of persistence/ grit, leadership, curiosity, initiative and social/ cultural awareness. Our students are chosen to participate as Employtech Fellows based on their aptitude for the Character Qualities, and the curriculum and learning experiences incorporate the Competencies skills throughout the process.
